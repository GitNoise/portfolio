01

Erosion of European Democracy

V-Dem · data exploration · explained.media

A V-Dem exploration: a series of views built to answer different questions rather than one chart. Europe's democratic high point was 2011; since then, 33 of 39 European countries have lost ground. A diverging heatmap makes year-on-year change scannable by country. A slope chart widens the lens to the full Europe-and-Caucasus region: 32 countries backsliding, 11 improving, between 2012 and 2022.

03

Infrastructure Security Map

Omny · freelance · fixed-scope

The Zones and Conduits diagram inside Omny's security platform: a live network map that automatically categorises assets and visualises their communication pathways, built to enforce and audit segmentation under the ISA/IEC 62443 industrial cybersecurity standard. Built in Svelte and D3 with a custom treemap layout algorithm for nesting multiple asset levels inside zones, plus zoom, pan, and dependency highlighting. Delivered freelance, concept to production.

Svelte · D3 · custom treemap layout
Confidential Client work under NDA. Abstracted walkthrough of the layout strategy and interaction design available on a call.

Anomaly Front Page

Confidential Much of my best work supports internal decision-making and can't be shown publicly. Happy to discuss architecture, design decisions, and my role.
Spotify · product · with data science · the good stuff is under NDA; ask me about it

The problem: surfacing the anomalies that matter from terabytes of mostly time-series data.

The approach: a monitoring tool structured as a newspaper front page, auto-generated text turns detected outliers into ranked, readable stories, some shared across teams, some personalised, each linked to a root-cause view.

About

Data visualisation is never neutral. Every dataset has boundaries someone drew, and every tool carries the assumptions of its design. That belief shapes the work I choose.

My background combines software engineering, journalism, machine learning, and disaster management: fields that meet wherever data and human consequence intersect. I've built humanitarian knowledge systems for the United Nations, Sweden's most-viewed COVID dashboard for Aftonbladet, and the financial analytics tools used across Spotify Finance. Today I lead the visualisation layer at Datastory, a platform for public-interest data storytelling.

Most of that work happens inside organisations and can't be shown publicly; the independent projects here show the range of what I build. I work best where off-the-shelf BI tools stop being enough, collaborating closely with researchers, data scientists, and journalists. In senior roles I've led teams, mentored developers, and taught data visualisation courses.

I selectively work with NGOs, mission-driven organisations, and teams handling complex or consequential data, in Sweden and internationally. Alongside building tools, I organise conversations about who data serves and how visualisation practice shapes understanding. I work in English, Swedish, Polish, and French.
